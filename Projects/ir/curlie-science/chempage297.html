Welcome to Eurachem


Image courtesy of SP, Sweden

Welcome to Eurachem, a network of organisations in Europe having the objective of establishing a system for the international traceability of chemical measurements and the promotion of good quality practices. It provides a forum for the discussion of common problems and for developing an informed and considered approach to both technical and policy issues. It provides a focus for analytical chemistry and quality related issues in Europe.

Recent News

  • Eurachem 2019 Measurement Uncertainty Workshops - Abstract deadline extended

    The organisers have extended the closing date for Abstracts for the Eurachem International Workshop on "Uncertainty from sampling and analysis for accredited laboratories". The workshop is kindly hosted by BAM at BAM headquarters, Unter den Eichen, Berlin, Germany,on 19-20 November 2019.

    Registration is via the web page at  www.eurachem.org/MU2019. [Operated by Cognito Forms]

    Abstracts can be submitted at www.eurachem.org/AbstractsMU2019 [Separate website] until 15 October 2019.

    Find out more on the workshop page >>

     

  • Eurachem guide "Setting Target Measurement Uncertainty" available in Spanish

    Thanks to hard work from our colleagues at Eurachem-España and Eurolab-España, the Eurachem guide "Setting and Using Target Uncertainty in Chemical Measurement" is now available for download from the Eurachem site in Spanish.

    The guide shows how to set a maximum admissible uncertainty, or “target uncertainty”, to check whether the measurement uncertainty is fit for the intended purpose.

    Download the translation here >>

     

     

  • Eurachem leaflet "Traceability of Analytical Results" now available in German

    Thanks to our colleagues at Eurolab-D in Germany, the Eurachem information leaflet "Traceability of Analytical Results" is now additionally available in German. The leaflet gives a short introduction to the the topic of metrological traceability.

    Download the leaflet here >>

     

  • Eurachem Validation Guide review: User feedback requested

    The 2nd edition of the guide ‘The Fitness for Purpose of Analytical Methods: A Laboratory Guide to Method Validation and Related Topics’ was published in 2014. Eurachem guides are reviewed five years after publication to assess the need for revision. As part of this process the Method Validation Working Group is seeking feedback from users of the guide.

    Click here (https://www.surveymonkey.co.uk/r/3V283RH) to access a short survey to give us your views on the guide. The survey should take no more than 15 minutes to complete. The closing date for responses is 20 September 2019.

     

  • New Supplementary guidance - Blanks in Method Validation

    The Eurachem Method Validation Working Group is pleased to announce a new, short, guide to the use of blanks in method validation. This is a short supplement to our guide on  Method validation, and describes the different types of blanks which may be used during method validation. It provides guidance for validation in situations where it may be difficult to obtain a suitable blank matrix.

    Download the Supplement here >>
