Disease Models & Mechanisms (DMM) is an online Open Access journal focusing on the use of model systems to better understand, diagnose and treat human disease.

The primary aim of DMM is to promote human health by inspiring collaboration between basic and clinical researchers in translational science. The journal is committed to presenting rigorously peer-reviewed research that has significant translational impact. The interdisciplinary nature of DMM means that a diverse range of diseases, approaches and models fall within its broad scope. DMM is guided by an international team of expert research-active Editors, led by Monica Justice and Ross Cagan, and supported by an outstanding Editorial Advisory Board.

Editor's choice

Valerie Brunton and colleagues report that deleting p53 from endothelial cells reliably leads to angiosarcoma, establishing a novel transplantable model of this rare cancer.

Also in this issue, Dominique Gaugier's group analysed kidney and fat tissue transcriptomes in rats to understand the shared aetiology of obesity and hypertension.  

Recent highlights

A new review by Emma Brown and Sarah Bohndiek discusses the potential of photoacoustic imaging as a non-invasive tool to visualise features of the tumour microenvironment.

We also highlight a new At a glance poster article on organoids as models of disease by Madeline Lancaster and Meritxell Huch.  

 

 

News from DMM

DMM is delighted to announce the appointment of new Editorial Board members Susumu Hirabayashi (Imperial College London, UK), Yun Li (SickKids, Toronto, Canada), Rachel K. Miller (McGovern Medical School, TX, USA) and Eirini Trompouki (Max Planck Institute of Immunobiology and Epigenetics, Freiburg, Germany). 

DMM is pleased to announce a new partnership with the Publons Reviewer Recognition Service. This will allow reviewers to easily track and verify every review by choosing to add the review to their Publons profile when completing the DMM review submission form. Publons makes it simple for reviewers to showcase their peer review contributions in a format that can be included in job and funding applications (without breaking reviewer anonymity).
