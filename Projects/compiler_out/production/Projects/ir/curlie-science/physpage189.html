    Physical Review Research has published its first research articles

    Read the first issue as it develops. Physical Review Research (PRResearch) is an open access, peer-reviewed, international journal covering the whole spectrum of research topics of interest to the physics community, and offering the Physical Review experience and quality you know and trust. To allow all authors an opportunity to experience this exciting new offering, APS is waiving open access publication charges (APCs) in 2019. Learn more, sign up for alerts, and submit your research today!

    ANNOUNCEMENT

    Meet Our New RMP Editor for Nuclear Physics

    August 1, 2019

    Roxanne Springer has a Ph.D. in theoretical physics from Caltech and is currently a professor of physics at Duke University. Her research interests are effective field theories of QCD and weak interactions. She was in the chair line of the Southeastern Section of the APS from 2010-2014, on the Executive Committee of the Division of Nuclear Physics (DNP) from 2015-2017, and currently serves on the APS DNP Anti-harassment Committee. She is a Fellow of the APS.

    In Memory of Bruno Eckhardt
    August 27, 2019

    It is with great sadness that we announce the unexpected passing of our dear colleague Bruno Eckhardt. Bruno was born March 25, 1960 and he died on August 7, 2019. He had served as Associate Editor for Physical Review E since 2007.

    Raissa D’Souza appointed Lead Editor of Physical Review Research
    June 26, 2019

    The American Physical Society is delighted to announce Raissa D’Souza, Professor at the University of California, Davis departments of Computer Science and Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, has been appointed Lead Editor of Physical Review Research. She joins fellow Lead Editors Nicola Spaldin and Jian-Wei Pan on the editorial leadership team of APS’ new open access, multidisciplinary journal, which is now open for submissions.

    Jian-Wei Pan appointed Lead Editor of Physical Review Research
    June 11, 2019

    Jian-Wei Pan, Professor of Physics at the University of Science and Technology of China in Hefei, has been appointed to one of the Lead Editor roles for Physical Review Research, the new open access, multidisciplinary journal, announced by APS earlier this year and opening for submissions this month. APS will soon be announcing additional Editor and Editorial Board member appointments. Read more here.
